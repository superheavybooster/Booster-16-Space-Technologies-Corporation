SkyLynx-1 First Stages

Reusable first stage components

Main Body Structure

Constructed from advanced carbon composite materials, the main body provides structural integrity while minimizing weight. Features integrated avionics bays and propellant tank systems.

Main Engine Assembly

High-thrust liquid fuel engine with deep throttling capability and gimbal control for precise trajectory adjustments. Features advanced cooling systems for extended burn times.

Landing System

Retractable carbon-composite landing legs with hydraulic dampening systems. Designed for multiple reuses with minimal refurbishment between flights.

Aerodynamic Control Surfaces

Titanium grid fins for precise atmospheric control during descent. Deployable immediately after stage separation for controlled reentry and landing.

SkyLynx-1 Second Stages

Upper stage and payload deployment systems

Upper Stage Structure

Lightweight aluminum-lithium alloy construction with integrated propellant tanks. Designed for maximum payload volume while maintaining structural integrity during high-G maneuvers.

Vacuum-Optimized Engine

High-efficiency vacuum engine with extended nozzle for optimal performance in space. Capable of multiple restarts for precise orbital insertion and trajectory adjustments.

Payload Fairing

Carbon composite fairing with acoustic damping system to protect delicate payloads. Jettison system ensures clean separation without imparting significant forces on the payload.

Avionics and Control Systems

Redundant flight computers, inertial navigation systems, and communication equipment. Provides autonomous guidance, navigation, and control throughout the mission.

Completed | Failure

The first flight of SkyLynx-1, Communi-One, didn't go as planned: the main engine failed to ignite at liftoff. SkyLynx-1 Flight Test 2

Completed | Partial Success

November 13, 2025 ‚Ä¢ Cape Canaveral

The second flight test of SkyLynx-1. The initial ascent went well with our new first stage engine and 4 SRBs igniting successfully. Like last timem something visibly ripped off but the rocket still continued. After that we had an off-nominal SRB separation with the SRBs colliding with each other but fortunately they didn't hit the first stage. Stage separation was after that which went well and even tho the second stage had some oversteering issues at first, engineers limited the gimbal on the second stage and it was able to be stabalized. During the second stage's burn the vehicle passed the apoapsis beofre SECO (Second Engine Cut Off). That meant that the vehicle would have to pitch up before the apoapsis went too far back in the orbit. The flight software though failed to compensate with that. After that the second stage reached the target apoapsis and shut down its engine but by then it was too late and the periapsis was the ground. The second stage then proceeded to reenter the Earth's atmosphere shortly after and burned up during reentry. Due to that happening we also didn't get to deploy our satellite that was planned to deploy in this mission. The rocket performed excelently in flight test 2 and we collected a lot of data. We are hoping that on flight 3 the flight software will be fixed and we're gonna get to orbit.

SkyLynx-1 Flight Test 1

Completed | Partial Success

November 9, 2025 ‚Ä¢ Cape Canaveral

The first flight test of SkyLynx-1. The vehicle lifted off nominally with the main engine and all 4 SRBs running normally. At T+55 seconds into the flight something was visibly ripped off the rocket. After that though the 4 SRBs separated nominally and flew away. After that though at T+1 minute and 7 seconds the main engine's gimbal locked at a weird placement and the gimbal was very limited. Although that didn't stop the rocket as it got to stage separation where the second engine started nominally but the second stage then started spinning out of control and at T+4 minutes and 24 seconds the FTS was triggered and the second stage was destroyed. The first stage and boosters had a hard splashdown downrange in the Atlantic ocean. We collected a lot of data from this flight and we hope to have a successful flight next time.

FUN FACT: This flight took 2 attempts to launch due to a problem with the flight software!
